# v0.1 Scope and Boundary

This document defines the scope, non-goals, adoption boundary, review expectations,
non-canon posture, and required packet pieces for the v0.1 release of
AI Source Verification (`ai-source-verification`).

It closes issue #1 ("Define v0.1 scope and boundary").

## Status

This is a **candidate** document. It is exploratory, version-controlled, and
reviewable. It is not an adopted standard and does not bind any downstream
consumer until reviewed and adopted through the governance path described in
`CONTRIBUTING.md`.

## Scope

v0.1 defines the **minimum executable unit** for AI source verification: a
reviewable, testable, agent-operable **verification packet** that captures
provenance, attestation, and review expectations for AI-mediated work.

In scope for v0.1:

- A single JSON schema describing a verification packet
  (`schemas/ai-source-verification-v0.1.json`).
- One valid example
  (`examples/verification-packet-v0.1.example.json`).
- One valid fixture
  (`fixtures/valid/verification-packet-v0.1.valid.json`).
- One invalid fixture
  (`fixtures/invalid/verification-packet-v0.1.invalid.json`).
- A receipt template
  (`receipts/ai-source-verification-v0.1-packet-receipt.md`).
- A prior-art survey (`docs/prior-art.md`).
- This boundary document (`docs/v0.1-boundary.md`).

Out of scope for v0.1 (deferred to later versions):

- A normative trust policy or trust registry.
- Cryptographic signature envelopes (e.g. Sigstore, C2PA manifests) embedded
  in the packet. v0.1 describes *where* signatures and receipts live, not how
  they are produced.
- Automated validation tooling beyond fixture-based shape checks.
- Multi-packet workflows, batch verification, or graph-level provenance.
- Adoption criteria and conformance programs.

## Non-goals

- This repo is not a universal standard.
- This repo does not claim ownership of the broader field or its terminology.
- This repo does not canonize HUMMBL/BaseN/Ownward concepts unless explicitly
  marked and audited.
- This repo must not include private, internal, or secret operational content.
- v0.1 does not assert that a packet is *true*; it asserts that a packet is
  *well-formed and reviewable*.

## Adoption boundary

v0.1 is intended for:

- Contributors evaluating the schema against real AI-mediated work.
- Reviewers performing human review of candidate packets.
- Agents producing or consuming packets in non-production, exploratory
  pipelines.

v0.1 is **not** intended for:

- Production trust decisions.
- Cross-organizational conformance claims.
- Legal, evidentiary, or compliance assertions.

Any consumer that adopts v0.1 must treat packets as **candidate artifacts**
until a reviewer signs the receipt.

## Review expectations

Every v0.1 packet must be reviewable by a human. A packet is considered
**reviewable** when:

1. It validates against
   `schemas/ai-source-verification-v0.1.json`.
2. Its `verificationManifest` identifies the work, version, and scope.
3. Its `verificationContract` records `sourceType`, `provenanceChain`,
   `attestationSpec`, `trustPolicy`, and `verificationStatus`.
4. Its `authority` records who produced the packet and on what basis.
5. Its `receiptRequirements` records what a reviewer must sign before the
   packet may be treated as reviewed.

A packet that validates but cannot satisfy review expectations 1-5 is
**well-formed but not reviewable** and must not be promoted to a reviewed
state.

## Non-canon posture

Nothing in v0.1 is canon. Specifically:

- The schema is a **candidate schema**, not a normative specification.
- The vocabulary in `docs/prior-art.md` is borrowed from public prior art and
  is not redefined as proprietary here.
- The examples and fixtures are illustrative, not authoritative.
- The receipt template is a review aid, not a legal instrument.

Adoption of any v0.1 artifact as canon requires a separate, reviewed
governance path outside the scope of this document.

## Required packet pieces

A v0.1 verification packet must contain, at minimum:

| Piece | Field | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Schema version | `schemaVersion` | Identifies the schema the packet conforms to. |
| Packet status | `packetStatus` | Records the lifecycle state of the packet. |
| Manifest | `verificationManifest` | Identifies the work: `id`, `title`, `version`, `scope`. |
| Authority | `authority` | Records who produced the packet and on what basis. |
| Contract | `verificationContract` | Records `sourceType`, `provenanceChain`, `attestationSpec`, `trustPolicy`, `verificationStatus`. |
| Receipt requirements | `receiptRequirements` | Records what a reviewer must sign and how. |

A packet missing any required piece is invalid by definition; see
`fixtures/invalid/verification-packet-v0.1.invalid.json` for the canonical
invalid example.
